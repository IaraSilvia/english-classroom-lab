# Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:

- Identify common emotions in English.
- Describe how they feel using simple sentences.
- Expand their vocabulary related to feelings.
- Express themselves creatively through visual representation.
- Develop confidence in speaking English.
- Practice empathy by listening to classmates' experiences.

---

# Materials

- Emotions & Emojis worksheet (PDF)
- Colored pencils or markers
- Whiteboard (optional)

---

# Vocabulary

Suggested vocabulary:

- Happy 😊
- Sad 😢
- Angry 😠
- Excited 🤩
- Tired 😴
- Nervous 😰
- Scared 😨
- Confused 😕
- Proud 😌
- Surprised 😲
- Calm 😌
- Worried 😟
- Bored 😒
- Lonely 😔
- Hopeful 😊

Teachers may adapt the vocabulary according to the students' level.

---

# Warm-up (5 minutes)

Ask students simple questions such as:

- How are you today?
- How do you feel on Mondays?
- What makes you happy?
- What makes you nervous?

Encourage students to answer using complete sentences whenever possible.

Example:

> I feel happy today because the weather is nice.

---

# Main Activity (15–20 minutes)

Distribute the worksheet.

Students should:

1. Create emojis representing different emotions.
2. Color or personalize each emoji.
3. Write the corresponding emotion in English.
4. Think of situations in which they experience each feeling.

Allow creativity and encourage students to make each emoji unique.

---

# Speaking Practice (10–15 minutes)

Invite students to share one or more emojis with the class.

Suggested questions:

- Why did you choose this emoji?
- When do you feel like this?
- Can you remember a situation?
- What usually makes you feel this way?

Encourage respectful listening and positive interaction.

---

# Reflection

Finish the lesson with a short discussion.

Possible questions:

- Which emotion was the easiest to describe?
- Which one was the most difficult?
- Did you discover something new about your classmates?
- How can understanding emotions improve communication?

---

# Teacher Notes

During the classroom application of this activity, students naturally began sharing personal experiences.

Although the worksheet was originally designed as a vocabulary exercise, it became an opportunity for meaningful conversations, laughter and moments of empathy.

Teachers are encouraged to allow these discussions to happen naturally while maintaining a respectful and welcoming classroom environment.

---

# Possible Extensions

## Pair Work

Students interview a partner using the emojis.

Example:

- When do you feel excited?
- What makes you feel proud?

---

## Writing Activity

Students write five sentences using different emotions.

Example:

- I feel excited when I travel.
- I feel nervous before an exam.

---

## Role Play

Students act out an emotion without speaking.

The rest of the class guesses:

> "You are surprised!"

---

## Homework

Create a new emoji representing an emotion not discussed in class.

Write:

- the emotion
- a sentence
- a real-life example

---

# Assessment

Students demonstrate their learning by:

- Using appropriate emotion vocabulary.
- Participating in discussions.
- Expressing ideas in English.
- Listening respectfully to classmates.
- Completing the worksheet.

Assessment should prioritize communication and participation rather than grammatical accuracy.

---

# Educational Approach

This lesson is based on a communicative approach that values interaction, creativity and authentic language use.

The activity encourages students to connect vocabulary with real-life experiences, making learning more meaningful and memorable.
